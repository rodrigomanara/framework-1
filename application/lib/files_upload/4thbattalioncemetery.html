Visiting Gallipoli. ANZAC  - 4th Battalion Parade Ground Cemetery

V Beach Helles Map Anzac map
Loading...

The 4th Battalion of the AIF Australian Imperial came New South Wales. The small cemetery which bears its name lies on the south eastern side Braund’s Hill in the area of Bridges Road that ran from Wire Gully back down into Shrapnel Valley and thence to the beach. In the early fighting members of the 4th battlation were buried here and the plot was enlarged after the campaign by drawing in nearby burials from the 3rd and 22nd Battalion cemeteries.


Visit: Rating: Moderate, with an optional fairly steep climb. Total distance about 2km

The cemetery contains 116 First World War burials, 7 of them unidentified. It can be seen from the area of Lone Pine and Johnston’s Jolly and can only be reached on foot.  

By far the easiest option is to take the signed footpath from the road opposite Johnston’s Jolly Cemetery, just to the north of Lone Pine. This is a moderate descent taking about ten minutes, openingon to the plateau behind Braund’s Hill.

From here there is choice of either retracing your steps back up to the road or otherwise continuing down the slope to the south west into Shrapnel Valley, folloeing this path to emerge at Shrapnel Valley cemetery. If you take this route then unless you intend to climb back up again you will need to make arrangements to be met by vehicle at the bottom.
